A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Indian President A P J Abdul Kalam (L) gives a lifetime achievement award to Dev Anand during the 50th National film awards ceremony in New Delhi, December 2003. Dev Anand was awarded with the Dadasaheb Phalke Award.

Here's a time line of Missile Man of India's life:

  • 1931- He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering.
  • 1960- After graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology, , Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation as a scientist after becoming a member of the Defence Research & Development Service (DRDS).
  • 1963- he visited NASA's Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia; Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland; and Wallops Flight Facility
  • 1965- Kalam had first started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO.
  • 1970- Kalam also directed two projects, Project Devil and Project Valiant, which sought to develop ballistic missiles from the technology of the successful SLV programme.
  • 1969- Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1971- Kalam made an effort to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects, both of which proved to be successful.
  • 1980- Successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980.
  • 1998- Along with cardiologist Soma Raju, Kalam developed a low cost coronary stent, named the "Kalam-Raju Stent".
  • 2002- Kalam served as the 11th president of India, succeeding K. R. Narayanan. He won the 2002 presidential election with an electoral vote of 922,884, surpassing the 107,366 votes won by Lakshmi Sahgal. His term lasted from 25 July 2002, to 25 July 2007
  • 2012- The duo designed a rugged tablet computer for health care in rural areas, which was named the "Kalam-Raju Tablet".
  • 2012- On 27 July 2015, Kalam travelled to Shillong to deliver a lecture on "Creating a Livable Planet Earth" at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. While climbing a flight of stairs, he experienced some discomfort, but was able to enter the auditorium after a brief rest.
